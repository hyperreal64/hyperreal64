The [Elder Scrolls official Twitter account](https://twitter.com/ElderScrolls/status/1344674735901339648) posted the image below, saying: "Transcribe the past and map the future." The three lights placed on the map are supposed to be clues as to the setting of TES VI.

![TES VI Clue](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/Eqk-iwuWMAUsXbW?format=jpg&name=4096x4096)

The top left light is right at the location of the Katariah, the Emperor's flagship moored in the Solitude inlet during the events of TES V: Skyrim. This is where the Emperor is assassinated by the Dark Brotherhood, at the hands of the [Last Dragonborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonborn). This could symbolize the death of the Empire.

The bottom-most light is clearly indicating Hammerfell. I'm not sure if the compass needle has any relevance to the clue. The tall cliff structure in the upper left edge of the image may be significant. Tall cliff ~= High Rock?

The top right light is more obscure to me. It would seem to have something to do with the coins next to it. The coins used throughout the Empire are known as "Septims", after [Tiber Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tiber_Septim), the founder of the first Empire. The [Septim bloodline](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septim_Dynasty) died out after Martin Septim used the [Amulet of Kings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amulet_of_Kings) to become an avatar of [Akatosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akatosh), sacrificing himself to end the [Oblivion Crisis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oblivion_Crisis) in 3E 433.

The coin on top of the pile depicts the [Wolf of Solitude](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Solitude). The coin directly underneath it seems to depict the two moons, Jode (Masser) and Jone (Secunda). The inscription on this coin reads "The two..." but is cut off at the top. Why are the depictions on these coins significant?

In 4E 98 (102 years before the events if TES V), the two moons, Masser and Secunda, disappeared from the sky for two years until the Thalmor claimed to have brought them back. The two moons are an integral part of Khajiit culture, so this gave the Aldmeri Dominion considerable influence over the Khajiit. While the Empire's influence in Elsweyr declined, the Elsweyr Confederacy split into the two kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine and became client states of the Aldmeri Dominion.

During the events of TES V, Solitude is the Empire's seat of power in Skyrim. In 4E 175 (25 years before TES V: Skyrim), the Empire signed the [White-Gold Concordat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:White-Gold_Concordat), a peace treaty with the Aldmeri Dominion, which ended the [Great War](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_War). The conditions of the treaty included banning the worship of Talos throughout the Empire, disbanding the [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blades), and giving a large portion of southern Hammerfell to the Aldmeri Dominion. Talos worhship is a big part of Nordic culture, so these conditions were unacceptable to many Nords and subsequently provoked a rebellion. Ulfric Stormcloak incited the rebellion when he murdered High-King Torygg at the Blue Palace in Solitude. During the events of TES V: Skyrim, Ulfric and the Stormcloaks (based in Windhelm) engage in a civil war against General Tullius and the Empire (based in Solitude). Regardless of whether the player sides with the Empire or the Stormcloaks, the civil war significantly weakens both Skyrim and the Empire. It's interesting to note that among the conditions of the White-Gold Concordat, the AD specifically demands the disbanding of the Blades, who are sworn to protect the Dragonborn Emperors of Tamriel. The Septim bloodline and the Amulet of Kings were destroyed to end the Oblivion Crisis, and the Thalmor actively persecute the Blades and the Last Dragonborn during TES V. The AD seems bent on diminishing the Empire's power by severing its ties to the Dragon God Akatosh. With the civil war in Skyrim and the Empire in disarray, the Aldmeri Dominion has a strategic advantage.

The Wolf of Solitude and Two Moons coins could represent the idea that the Empire and the two kingdoms of Elsweyr are in the pocket of the Aldmeri Dominion. It's hard to make out what the coins underneath these two depict, but maybe a starting point to narrow it down could be to check the lore with regard to which nations the Aldmeri Dominion has "in their pocket" by the end of TES V.

So, starting from the top east-most coin and going south-westward, a lore-consistent narrative of the clue could be something like the followng: The Empire, Elsweyr, and whatever nations the other coins represent, are in the pocket of the Aldmeri Dominion, which leads to the death of the Empire. The south-western-most light then would indicate that the next plot point in the war against the AD is in Hammerfell.

Assuming this is (relatively) correct, it makes me wonder whether the person who hired the Dark Brotherhood to assassinate Emperor Titus Mede II might have been an agent of the Thalmor. His name is [Amaund Motierre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Amaund_Motierre), and all that is currently known about him is that he is part of a powerful Breton family established in Cyrodiil, and he paid the Dark Brotherhood in advance with an amulet that is given exclusively to members of the Elder Council.
